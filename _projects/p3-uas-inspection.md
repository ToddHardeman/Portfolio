Aircraft inspections are among the most critical — and most time-intensive — maintenance activities in aviation. For large-frame aircraft like the C-17 Globemaster III, traditional inspections require personnel to physically access difficult or dangerous positions, consuming significant man-hours and introducing human-factor risks.

This project pioneered a fundamentally different approach. Working in collaboration with Skydio, the first UAS-based inspection methodology for the C-17 platform was developed — deploying autonomous drone technology with automated visual analysis to perform comprehensive aircraft checks that previously required manual access.

The initiative required extensive coordination across maintenance, safety, and leadership stakeholders to gain approval for an unprecedented operational methodology. Technical rigor was applied to document the process, analyze UAS-captured imagery data, and establish repeatable inspection protocols that could be validated and scaled.

The results were transformative: inspection duration was reduced by 92%, personnel safety was dramatically improved by eliminating high-risk physical access requirements, and an enterprise-wide precedent for automated maintenance was established. The methodology has since been positioned for adoption across the broader fleet.
