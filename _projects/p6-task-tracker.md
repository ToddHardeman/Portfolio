The partnership between military units and academic institutions created a complex task management challenge: multiple organizations, different processes, and no shared visibility into training progress, deadlines, or bottlenecks. The result was persistent training backlogs and accountability gaps.

This project co-developed a purpose-built digital task tracking application to solve that problem. Extensive stakeholder engagement with both military and academic partners shaped the requirements — ensuring the system served both organizational cultures and operational needs.

The Power Apps interface provides intuitive task creation, assignment, and status tracking for users across both organizations. Power Automate handles notification logic, escalation workflows, and status transitions automatically. MS Lists provides the structured data foundation that enables reporting and cross-organizational visibility.

The deployment eliminated the training backlogs that had been accumulating before implementation, established clear accountability mechanisms, and created a shared operational picture across the military-academic partnership. This project exemplifies the ability to bridge institutional boundaries through thoughtful technology design.
