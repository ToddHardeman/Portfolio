When a commissioned officer-level vacancy created a reporting gap at the 18th Air Force level, the stakes were high: general officers making strategic decisions depend on accurate, timely data — and that data pipeline had broken down.

Rather than waiting for a personnel solution, this project engineered an automated reporting system that restored and improved that pipeline. The work began with deep stakeholder engagement to understand what data general officers required, at what cadence, and in what format — translating strategic leadership requirements into technical specifications.

Power Apps was used to build data collection interfaces that standardized input from contributing units. Power Automate orchestrated the aggregation, validation, and distribution workflows — ensuring data accuracy through automated checks rather than manual review. The resulting executive-ready reports were delivered automatically on schedule, without manual intervention.

The system didn't just fill a vacancy — it improved the process. By eliminating manual aggregation errors and standardizing data formats, accuracy increased measurably. This project demonstrates the ability to operate at the intersection of strategic leadership and technical execution — understanding what leadership needs and engineering systems that reliably deliver it.
