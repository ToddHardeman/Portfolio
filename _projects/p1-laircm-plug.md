There was a systemic problem with the KC-46's Large Infrared Countermeasures system: when water infiltrated the turret housing, the system failed completely at a cost of $887,553 per occurrence. This was a significant operational and financial vulnerability across the fleet.

Rather than continuing to repair failures after they occurred, the mission became designing a preventive solution. Extensive materials research and testing were conducted to identify compounds capable of forming a reliable, long-lasting seal under the environmental conditions experienced by operational aircraft. After multiple design iterations using Fusion 360, the solution was prototyped and field-validated.

The resulting plug — a precision-engineered inlet/exhaust port cover — costs approximately $25 to produce and can be manufactured at scale. Field testing over 51 days demonstrated zero turret losses on protected aircraft versus 100% loss rates on unprotected aircraft. The findings were documented in a comprehensive white paper and submitted for enterprise-wide implementation across Air Mobility Command.

This project represents the convergence of hands-on aviation expertise, engineering design principles, and strategic thinking — turning a high-cost reactive problem into a proactive, near-zero-cost solution.
